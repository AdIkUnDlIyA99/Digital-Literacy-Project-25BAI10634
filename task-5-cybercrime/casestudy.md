# **UPI/ONLINE PAYMENT FRAUD**



UPI fraud is a rapidly growing form of cybercrime where attackers trick users into authorizing unauthorized transactions. It mainly exploits a lack of awareness rather than technical vulnerabilities.



A common scenario begins when a victim receives a call or message from someone pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or even a buyer/seller from an online marketplace. The fraudster builds trust and then claims that money will be transferred to the victim’s account. Instead of sending money, they initiate a “collect request” on a UPI app.



Step-by-step, the fraud typically occurs as follows:

First, the attacker contacts the victim with a convincing story. Second, they request the victim to approve a payment request or enter their UPI PIN under the false assumption of receiving money. Third, once the victim enters the PIN, the money is instantly debited from their account. In some cases, phishing links are also used to mimic legitimate apps and steal login credentials.



The most common targets include elderly individuals, new digital payment users, and people unfamiliar with online transaction processes. Small business owners and students are also frequently targeted due to high transaction activity.



The consequences can be severe. Victims often lose significant amounts of money within minutes, and recovery is difficult if not reported immediately. Beyond financial loss, victims may also experience stress, anxiety, and loss of trust in digital systems.



This case highlights the importance of digital awareness, as simple precautions can prevent such fraud.
